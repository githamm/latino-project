Charts: The State of the Latino Family

The State of the Latino Family, a national, wide-ranging poll by the W.K. Kellogg Foundation, explored attitudes within a demographic projected to make up nearly one-third of the U.S. population by 2060. Latinos currently account for 21 percent of Colorado’s 5.3 million residents and could become one-third of the state’s population by 2040.

About 1,000 randomly-selected Latinos were surveyed as part of the Kellogg Foundation's “America Healing” project to promote racial equity. The poll had a margin of error of 3.1 percentage points.

Latinos from a broad range of demographics were asked about a wide-array of topics including the economy, immigration, education, health care and crime. The poll showed Latinos were generally satisfied with various aspects of life in the United States, but also had strong concerns about the direction of the country, crime and anti-Latino, anti-immigration attitudes.

The following series of charts provides a look at Latino feelings broken out by high school or college graduates, citizens, undocumented immigrants and fourth generation citizens. It also offers a look at the diverse feelings between men and women on various topics.
